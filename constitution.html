GOVERNING DOCUMENT

Constitution

RUET GIS Club

Article I — Name of Organization Article II — Purpose of Organization Article III — Officers of the Club Article IV — Membership Article V — Advisory Committee Article VI — Amendments Article VII — Affiliation Article VIII — Dissolution

ARTICLE I — Name of Organization

Section 01: The name of this organization will be “RUET Geographic Information Systems Club.” The use of the acronym GIS Club is permitted for advertising purposes.

Section 02: In any event, the organization's name must be written as “RUET Geographic Information Systems Club” while representing Rajshahi University of Engineering & Technology (RUET) through projects, conferences, workshops, and any other involvements.

Section 03: In any exceptional scenario where the full name may not be applied through proper measures — such as merchandise, kits, logo, or recognition on collaborative practices — the acronym can be applied and written only as “RUET GIS Club.”

Section 04: The name of the organization may be subject to change by vote amongst the organization's founding officers and head advisors. The vote must be majority favored and approved by the head advisors before submitting any changes to the organization.

Section 05: To change the name of the organization, the approval of the founding president is compulsory. If this condition is not met, nothing mentioned in Section 04 will make any difference. This section will never be changed in any kind of vote.

ARTICLE II — Purpose of Organization

The RUET Geographic Information Systems Club is an academic organization of Rajshahi University of Engineering & Technology that deals with the application of GIS and RS in daily life, activities, and various research fields — discovery and application of new technologies, drone-based mapping, statistical analysis with maps, and spatial & temporal observation of data — alongside student/teacher-led info sessions, seminars, and workshops related to GIS applications. The RUET GIS Club works to contribute to the Sustainable Development Goals (SDG), integrating SDG 06: Clean Water and Sanitation, SDG 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities, and SDG 13: Climate Action into all of its activities.

ARTICLE III — Officers of the Club

Section 01 — Committee Members: Elected officers will be President, Vice President, General Secretary, Assistant General Secretary, Treasurer, Assistant Treasurer, Event Planning and Management Secretary, Assistant Event Planning and Management Secretary, Art and Graphics Secretary, Assistant Art and Graphics Secretary, Media Secretary, Assistant Media Secretary, Research and Publication Secretary, Assistant Research and Publication Secretary, Volunteer Management Secretary, Assistant Volunteer Management Secretary, Office Secretary, and Assistant Office Secretary.

Section 02: The mentioned officers are responsible for all work and initiatives taken by the club. During an event, a separate group of organizers for that specific event can be formed by the elected officers, if necessary.

Section 03 — Roles and Responsibilities

President: Acts as the communication bridge between the club and RUET's faculty and administration, manages the whole team, and gives final input on decisions not settled by vote. Has the final say in disputes and decisions.

Vice President: Aids the president and performs their duties in absence. May perform the duties of other absent officers (excluding Treasurer) with three days' notice, or one week's notice for event-date absences. Must attend all off-campus field trips and half of on-campus events.

General Secretary: Maintains documents and records, organizes meetings, and keeps members informed of the club's progress and goals. Consults with the President and Vice President on decisive matters.

Joint Secretary: Supports the General Secretary and acts in their absence; manages member registration and recruitment across events and workshops.

Treasurer: In charge of the club's finances, expense tracking, and staying within budget; collaborates on fundraising for the organization's activities.

Office Secretary: Manages official documents, meetings and official sections of the club.

Assistant Office Secretary: Assists and, in absence, takes over office management duties.

Event Planning and Management Secretary: Organizes GIS-related events and activities, coordinates with other members, and collaborates with the Media Secretary on publicity. Manages interim teams formed during events.

Assistant Event Planning and Management Secretary: Assists and, in absence, takes over event planning duties.

Research and Workshop Secretary: Publishes documents and articles related to the club's interests, coordinates GIS research, and ensures research quality.

Assistant Research and Workshop Secretary: Assists and, in absence, takes over Research and Workshop Secretary's duties.

Media Secretary: Promotes the club and its activities through social media, newsletters, and flyers, and works with the Event Planning Secretary to promote events.

Assistant Media Secretary: Assists and, in absence, takes over promotional duties.

Graphics and Design Secretary: Creates designs and banners for events, and manages social media visuals. Responsible for the logo and any design changes; may form a volunteer team during events.

Assistant Graphics and Design Secretary: Assists and, in absence, takes over design duties.

GIS Resource Coordinator: Coordinates with all the departments in and out RUET, manages all resources and responsible for outside communication.

Volunteer Management Secretary: Recruits and manages volunteers, ensuring they are trained, supported, and contributing effectively.

Section 04: The founding committee serves six months; later committees serve one academic year. Departing members work closely with successors to ensure a smooth transfer of responsibilities.

Section 05 — Removal of an Officer

Part 01: A written request by at least three voting officers is submitted to the President or Vice President, with written notice sent to the officer in question to attend the next meeting.

Part 02: A two-thirds majority vote of the officers is required to remove an officer.

Part 03: An interim replacement may be appointed until an election or selection is held, requiring half the vote of present officers.

Section 06 — Election Procedure

New officers are appointed by election or selection, as preferred by the present committee. A 60% vote for each position is needed for appointment; if unopposed, the nominated candidate is directly selected. If 60% cannot be achieved, the elected president's vote is final. For the presidency itself, the advisory committee can veto the current president's decision, triggering a new election between the two nominees, decided by a 51% vote. The current president calls the election meeting at least one month before the current committee's term ends.

ARTICLE IV — Membership

The President, Vice President, or General Secretary can call a meeting to discuss any necessary topic, with at least one meeting held every month. Special meetings during events or emergencies may be requested by other officers directly to the President, or through the Vice President. All members are expected to attend meetings; if a member cannot attend, they must inform the President beforehand. Missing at least 60% of total meetings results in revoked membership.

ARTICLE V — Advisory Committee

An individual advisory committee, consisting only of RUET faculty members, sits alongside the student-elected committee. A faculty member on leave cannot remain on the advisory board but may rejoin after resuming their department role.

Section 01: The advisory committee serves as a resource body, providing advisory support for the officers and members of the club.

Section 02: The student organization advisor must be a full-time, salaried faculty or staff member not on leave during their advisor term.

Section 03: The advisory committee must be informed of any decision taken by the present committee and can combinedly veto a decision if necessary; the president may then present an alternate proposal but cannot proceed on the vetoed one.

ARTICLE VI — Amendments

Amendments to this constitution may be made at any regular meeting, provided notice of the proposed amendment was given two weeks prior to a vote. Amendments require a two-thirds vote of the voting members in attendance, are subject to final approval by the current committee, and must be presented to the advisory committee for final approval.

ARTICLE VII — Affiliation

RUET GIS Club can act as an affiliate with any international or national organization. During an affiliation period, no changes are made to the constitution unless absolutely necessary. Members can be involved in the affiliated program, and the club can recruit new members contractually for the program. The club can appoint an officer to manage the affiliated program, reporting directly to the president; in the absence of a selection, the Event Planning and Management Secretary plays this role. The club may form a new team for the affiliation program by direct selection of the president and general secretary; in case of disagreement, the president's opinion is respected.

ARTICLE VIII — Dissolution

In the event RUET GIS Club dissolves, all remaining funds and merchandise, after outstanding debts and claims are paid, are transferred to the advisory committee, which takes action for the remaining funds. The current president ensures the funds are distributed correctly, and the treasurer is responsible for the accounts during this event.
